Key Takeaway: SG: Approach is the single most predictive stat at Augusta. In 4 of the last 5 Masters, approach play had a larger influence on scoring than putting. None of the last 5 winners ranked inside the top 10 in SG: Putting during the tournament. Rory McIlroy actually lost strokes putting in 2025 and still won. The course neutralizes putting advantages due to extreme slopes (2.5% avg vs. 1.5% Tour avg) and 13–14 stimp speeds. Bet the iron game, not the flat stick.

03 Value Identification
Where the market is wrong this week:

1. Scheffler's price is too short. +485 implies ~17% win probability. DataGolf gives him 12.2%. He's 16th in SG:APP, hasn't played since the Players, and skipped Houston for the birth of his second child. His Augusta floor is elite (two wins) but the public is pricing him like 2024 Scheffler. He's not there right now.

2. DeChambeau is a public trap. Biggest liability at BetMGM. +1075 implies ~8.5%. His approach play on LIV is mid-tier, and Augusta is a second-shot course. Two T-6 finishes is a solid floor, not a win profile. The public loves the brand. The data says top 10, not green jacket.

3. Si Woo Kim at 49-1 is egregious. 4th in SG:T2G, 3rd in SG:APP, 1st in proximity on Tour. Made 7 of 8 Augusta cuts. The approach data alone puts him in the top 10 of the field. 49-1 implies ~2% win probability. His true probability based on form is closer to 3.5–4%.

4. Patrick Reed at 43-1. Five top 12 finishes in seven Masters since winning in 2018. Third last year. Two Euro Tour wins in 2026. The market treats him as a LIV afterthought. His Augusta-specific SG is elite. 43-1 is too long for a guy with this course history.

5. Adam Scott at 60-1 with the 2nd best approach on Tour. When seven straight winners entered top 30 in SG:APP, and Scott is currently 2nd, you're getting a champion-profile player at 60-1 because of his age. 21 cuts in 24 starts. Still top 20 in driving distance.

Implied Prob: ~17%
DataGolf Prob: 12.2%
Edge: -4.8% (negative)
The market gives Scheffler a 17% implied win probability. DataGolf's model gives him 12.2%. That's a 4.8% gap in the WRONG direction. He's 16th in SG:APP, hasn't played since the Players in mid-March, and skipped Houston for the birth of his second child. His Augusta floor is a top 5 (hence the top-5 bet being more justifiable), but the outright price is too short for his current form. You're betting on 2024 Scheffler at 2026 prices.

Bryson DeChambeau Outright +1075

Edge: -3.5% (negative)
Largest liability at BetMGM. The public loves Bryson's brand, his YouTube presence, and his 2024 U.S. Open win. But Augusta is a second-shot course and his LIV approach data is mid-tier at best. Two T-6 finishes is a solid floor, not a winner's ceiling. His implied probability is inflated by public action, not sharp money. Let the squares have him.

Rory McIlroy Outright +1150

Implied Prob: ~8%

T46 at the Players and described his own form as "unbelievably rusty." 46th in putting. SG metrics aren't aligned with a champion's form: 12th total, 16th approach, 46th putting. Amen Corner will test a compromised back. He's 1st in T2G on the season, but the last month paints a different picture. The public will bet Rory. Let them.

Jordan Spieth Outright 42-1

At 42-1, there are better options with stronger current form at longer odds (Kim, Reed, Adam Scott). Spieth's ceiling is a top 10, but the outright odds don't reflect how far his baseline game has slipped.

Ludvig Aberg Outright +1650

Implied Prob: ~5.7%

Aberg is talented and trending. Three straight top 5s. But he's 36th in SG:APP. At Augusta, where approach is the #1 predictor, that's a red flag the market is ignoring. CBS SportsLine model has him finishing top 5 only 25% of the time. The Players collapse is still recent memory. His top-10 bet (+154) is a better play than the outright if you want exposure. The outright at +1650 is priced too richly for a player whose most predictive stat is mid-tier.

Bankroll Structure:

Total deployment: 18.5 units across 15 bets.
Outright bets: 3.5 units (19% of card). High risk, high reward. Spread across 3 players to avoid concentration risk.
Top 5 bets: 5.0 units (27%). Higher hit rate. Schauffele and Fleetwood have genuine 25–35% top-5 probabilities.

Expected value: If picks hit at modeled rates, the card returns approximately +4.2 units on 18.5 units deployed, for a ~23% ROI. The outright longshots are the variance drivers. The placement bets provide the grind profit.
